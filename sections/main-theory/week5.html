Week 5: Measuring and Marking Out

Now we get hands-on: before any cutting, we need to accurately measure and mark our wood. Precision here means parts that fit well.

Units and Tools

We use millimeters (mm) for precision. Key tools include:

  • Ruler/Tape Measure: read carefully and note measurements in mm.
  • Try Square: draw accurate 90° lines from an edge.
  • Pencil: keep it sharp for fine marks.

Measuring Twice

"Measure twice, cut once" reminds us to double-check every mark before cutting.

Marking Out Steps

  1. Measure from a reference edge and mark a small tick.
  2. Repeat from another spot to confirm the distance.
  3. Join the marks with a try square.
  4. Mark an "X" on the waste side of the line.

Accuracy Tips

  • Start measuring from the ruler's zero mark.
  • Keep the ruler straight and aligned.
  • Clamp identical parts together when marking.
  • Use reference edges and templates when needed.
  • Have someone check your marks before cutting.
