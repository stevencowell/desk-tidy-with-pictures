Week 10: Finishing Touches and Presentation

In the final week, give your desk tidy a smooth finish and prepare to show the class what you have made. Sand any rough surfaces, apply paint or clear sealer in thin coats, and make sure every joint is secure. When presenting, speak clearly about your design choices and what you learned. Afterwards, tidy the workspace for the next class.

Finishing Steps

  • Sand surfaces smooth and remove all dust before finishing.
  • Apply finish in light coats, letting each coat dry fully.
  • Check that hardware is tight and glue joints are solid.

Presentation Tips

Show your sketch or plan, point out key features, and mention any challenges you overcame while making the project.

Week 10 Quiz

  1. Scenario: After assembling his desk tidy, a student notices a small gap in one of the joints but decides to apply finish anyway. The gap is still visible after finishing. What should he have done before finishing?




  2. Scenario: During his presentation, a student reads from his notes in a monotone and rarely looks up. How could he improve his presentation?




  3. Scenario: A student finishes painting his tidy the night before presentations. The next morning the paint feels dry, so he carries it to class and leaves fingerprints. What should he do differently next time?




  4. Scenario: After finishing his project, a student leaves sawdust, off‑cuts and tools on the bench. The next class cannot start their work. Why is cleaning the workspace an important part of the final steps?




  5. Scenario: To save time, a student applies one thick coat of clear sealer to his tidy. It drips and takes a long time to dry. What is a better finishing technique?




  6. Scenario: In his final presentation, a student forgets to bring his design sketch or plan. Why would including a sketch improve his presentation?




  7. Scenario: Pencil guide lines are still visible under the varnish on a student’s tidy. How could he prevent pencil marks from showing in the final product?




  8. Scenario: After finishing, a student attaches a hook by driving a screw directly into the finished timber without drilling. The wood splits. What should he have done before finishing?




  9. Scenario: A student’s presentation focuses only on what went well and doesn’t mention any difficulties. Why should he also discuss challenges he faced?




  10. Scenario: At the end of the project, leftover scrap wood is piled under the benches. Why is it important to clear away leftover materials after finishing?
