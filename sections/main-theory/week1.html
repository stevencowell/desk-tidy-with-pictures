Week 1: Introduction and Workshop Safety

Introduction

Welcome to the Desk Tidy project! In this first week, we introduce the project and focus on workshop safety. A desk tidy is a small wooden organiser that keeps your desk items neat. We're going to design and build one over the next 10 weeks. Before any cutting or building, safety is our top priority – a workshop has tools and equipment that can be dangerous if not used properly.

Workshop Safety Basics

Always pay attention to safety signs and warnings in the workshop
  • Listen to instructions: pay close attention to your teacher and follow their guidance at all times.
  • Wear PPE (Personal Protective Equipment): always wear safety glasses or goggles when cutting, sanding, or using any tools. Put on a workshop apron to protect your clothes and keep loose clothing in check. Sturdy shoes must be worn.
  • Tie back long hair and remove jewellery so nothing gets caught in tools.
  • No running or goofing around in the workshop.
  • Use tools correctly: only after you have been taught how to use them safely.
  • Keep a tidy workspace and clean up any spills.
  • Be patient and never rush; ask the teacher if you feel unsure.
  • Report problems immediately if a tool breaks or someone is injured.

Essential Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Safety glasses - Essential for all cutting and sanding tasks
Hard hat - Protects your head from falling objects
Work gloves - Protects hands from cuts and abrasions
Sturdy work boots - Closed-toe shoes are mandatory

Emergency Equipment and Fire Safety

Fire extinguisher - Know its location and how to use it
Fire blanket - Used to smother small fires
Fire exit - Always know your escape route

Workshop Equipment Safety

Woodwork vice - Securely clamp workpieces before cutting
Extension leads - Keep cords tidy and away from walkways
High visibility clothing - Makes you visible to others

Safety Hierarchy and Control Measures

Understanding the hierarchy of controls helps prevent accidents

By following these rules, we can prevent accidents and ensure the workshop is a safe, fun place to create your desk tidy. Safety may seem like common sense, but even experienced woodworkers follow strict safety rules every day. Let's build good habits from the start!
