Week 7: Attaching Hardware

With the desk tidy assembled, this week focuses on attaching small hardware like handles or hooks. Correct placement and screw technique keep your project sturdy.

Key Points

  • Mark hardware positions carefully before drilling.
  • Drill pilot holes to stop the timber splitting.
  • Select screws just long enough to hold securely.
  • Countersink if you want screw heads level with the surface.
  • Use a screwdriver that fits the screw head properly.
Week 7 Quiz

  1. Scenario: A student plans to attach a handle to the centre of a drawer front. They measure from the left edge across and end up off‑centre. How should they locate the correct centre point?




  2. Scenario: Mia wants to attach a hinge to a side panel that is 12 mm thick. She selects 40 mm screws and finds they poke through the inside. Which screw length would be most suitable?




  3. Scenario: Tom fastens a hook to his project using wood screws but does not pre‑drill. The wood splits. What could Tom do differently next time to prevent splitting?




  4. Scenario: Cara uses a large Phillips screwdriver on small slot‑head screws when attaching a latch. The driver slips and damages the screw heads. What is the best fix?




  5. Scenario: Jamal wants his screw heads to sit flush with the surface for a neat finish. How should he prepare the screw holes?




  6. Scenario: Sara installs hooks inside her tidy, but the nails come through the outside face. What mistake did she make, and how can she avoid it?




  7. Scenario: Ben uses 4 mm diameter screws to attach a bracket and drills 4 mm pilot holes so the screws slide in easily. The screws don’t bite into the wood. What size pilot holes should Ben drill?




  8. Scenario: Lily needs to drive a small screw near the edge. She holds the screw between her fingers and uses a cordless driver, but the bit slips dangerously close to her hand. How can she start the screw safely?




  9. Scenario: Mark marks handle positions with a pencil before sanding and painting, then finds the marks are gone when he’s ready to attach the hardware. How could he keep the positions visible?




  10. Scenario: Felix uses a drill to tighten a knob screw until it won’t turn any more and strips the thread. How can he prevent damaging the hardware?




Conclusion

Attaching hardware carefully ensures it functions well and the timber stays undamaged.
