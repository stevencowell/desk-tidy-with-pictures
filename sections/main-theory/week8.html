Week 8: Decorative Touches and Finishing

This week focuses on adding colour and protective coatings to give your desk tidy a finished look.

Carefully sand all surfaces smooth, then apply a primer if you plan to paint. After decorating, allow ample drying time before use.

Week 8 Quiz

  1. Scenario: A student paints their desk tidy without sanding first. The finish appears rough and uneven. What step should they take before painting to achieve a smooth finish?




  2. Scenario: Another student applies one thick coat of paint in the hope of finishing quickly. The paint drips and runs. What is a better approach?




  3. Scenario: Before painting, a student wonders whether to apply a primer. When is a primer useful?




  4. Scenario: A student is applying varnish in a small, closed room and begins to feel dizzy from the fumes. What should she have done to work safely?




  5. Scenario: After painting, a student leaves the tidy outside on a windy day. Dust and leaves stick to the wet paint. How can he prevent debris from ruining the finish?




  6. Scenario: A student stains her project and then immediately applies a clear varnish over the top. The stain mixes with the varnish and smears. What was the mistake?




  7. Scenario: After sanding with coarse paper, a student quickly applies paint without wiping away the dust. The finish is gritty. What should he have done after sanding?




  8. Scenario: A student paints his desk tidy while the handles and hinges are still attached. After drying, paint has built up around the hardware and it doesn’t move freely. What is the proper way to handle hardware during finishing?




  9. Scenario: To achieve a very smooth surface, a student uses the same coarse sandpaper throughout the sanding process. The finish still feels rough. How can he get a finer result?




  10. Scenario: After applying the final coat of paint, a student touches the surface an hour later to show a friend and leaves fingerprints. What should he do to avoid smudging the finish?
