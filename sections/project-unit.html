Desk Tidy Unit

The Desk Tidy unit introduces students to timber basics through a short design and build project. Over 10 weeks they plan and construct a small organiser while learning safe workshop practice and design thinking.

Summary & Duration

Duration: 10 Weeks (40-minute lessons, 4 per week)
Focus: Measuring and marking, basic hand tools, simple joints, surface preparation, finishing and evaluation.

Unit Overview

This Stage 4 project guides students through the design process from brief to evaluation. Using sustainably sourced timber, they create working drawings, prepare a cutting list and assemble their desk tidy with hand tools. Theory lessons explore materials, sustainability and safe work habits that support the practical work.

Learning Outcomes & Assessment

  • TE4-SDP-01: Explain how timber choice affects the design and construction of the organiser.
  • TE4-PDP-01: Apply design and production skills during planning and building.
  • TE4-MSC-01: Identify material properties to select the best timber.
  • TE4-DES-01: Develop sketches and working drawings for a Desk Tidy solution.
  • TE4-PPM-01: Plan and manage simple workshop procedures safely.

Assessment: Practical project – 60% | Folio & theory – 20% | Safe work practices – 20%
